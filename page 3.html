What Not to Do for Sustainable Transportation and Urban Planning

Please click these flipable cards

Avoid neglecting pedestrian infrastructure, as it can hinder walkability and accessibility, leading to increased reliance on vehicles and contributing to traffic congestion and pollution.
Refrain from implementing outdated or inefficient public transportation systems, as these can discourage usage and fail to provide viable alternatives to private vehicles, perpetuating environmental and congestion issues.
Avoid overlooking the importance of green spaces and natural habitats in urban planning, as this oversight can lead to the loss of biodiversity, exacerbate the urban heat island effect, and compromise the well-being of residents.
Do not prioritize car-centric design in urban planning, as this approach can lead to the proliferation of sprawling, auto-dependent communities, undermining efforts to promote sustainable modes of transportation and exacerbating environmental degradation.
Steer clear of disregarding the need for integrated land use and transportation planning, as this oversight can result in inefficient spatial layouts, increased travel distances, and the fragmentation of communities, leading to social and environmental challenges.
Avoid neglecting the integration of sustainable and resilient infrastructure solutions, as this oversight can make cities more vulnerable to the adverse impacts of climate change, jeopardizing the long-term viability and sustainability of urban communities.
