What to Do for Sustainable Transportation and Urban Planning

Promote Walkability: Invest in well-maintained pedestrian infrastructure to encourage walking and reduce reliance on cars, mitigating traffic congestion and air pollution.
Efficient Public Transit: Implement modern and efficient public transportation systems to provide accessible and sustainable alternatives to private vehicles, reducing carbon emissions and traffic congestion.
Integrate Green Spaces: Prioritize the inclusion of green spaces and natural habitats in urban planning to enhance biodiversity, mitigate the urban heat island effect, and improve the overall well-being of residents.
Prioritize Sustainable Design: Focus on sustainable and people-centric urban design to discourage car-centric communities, promoting sustainable transportation and reducing environmental degradation.
Integrated Planning: Emphasize integrated land use and transportation planning to create efficient spatial layouts, reduce travel distances, and foster cohesive communities, addressing social and environmental challenges.
Resilient Infrastructure: Integrate resilient infrastructure solutions to prepare cities for the adverse impacts of climate change, ensuring long-term sustainability and resilience for urban communities.
